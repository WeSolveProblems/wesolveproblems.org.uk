    BIG IDEAS FOR YOUNGER BRAINS

    WeSolveProblems brings big maths ideas to Years 7-10. Each Saturday, during term time, our PhD-led team of mathematicians coax you through mind-mangling arrays of advanced maths training at UCL.

    Enroll for 2018/19 NOW!

    WHO ARE WE?

    We Solve Problems

    a charity started by PhD mathematicians - brings the problem-solving and collaborative working skills, as well as the fun of advanced maths into the classroom.

    We train in MathsCircles

    (held at UCL as well as in individual schools) then pit mathematical wits in MathsBattles.

    No calculators, phones,

    or other technologies allowed, just brain cells, fingers and toes :)

    All tutors have at least

    a Masters degree in a maths area and are led by our own PhD trustees; professional mathematicians in their own right in a variety of fields.

    WHY STUDY MATHS?

    Fancy being a computer games designer, a forensic scientist, an architect, an air traffic controller, a climatologist, or an astronaut? You need excellent numerical literacy.

    Maths-based graduates earn significantly more than graduates from other disciplines - 5 years later they can be earning twice as much as art graduates.

    Maths is the basis of human life; written maths came before written language. 20,000 years ago cavemen notched bones to keep count of stuff and we’re still using the maths of our ancestors – Pythagoras wrote his famous Theorem 2,700 years ago!

    Sign up for WSP Maths Club in UCL

    WHAT DO YOU LEARN?

    This isn’t a school maths lesson. We do advanced maths that will enrich and extend your understanding, allowing you to think creatively and logically.

    t will help with your verbal reasoning, confidence, and presentation skills. Last year’s pupils said they were more confident in their maths skills with nearly 80% feeling more motivated at school: “The teachers challenged us and helped us think outside the box” and “I liked learning new maths skills I don’t learn at school”.

    t’s like opening a mathematical box of chocolates and trying to decide which flavour to each first! Will it be graph theory, maths paradoxes, game theory, visual algebra, tiling, invariants, algorithms…

    WHO CAN DO THIS?

    WSP@UCL is open to school years 7 – 10, but you do need mathematical confidence in order to understand and fully contribute to events.

    There’s no charge to attend our UCL events and everything is provided. All you need to do is be able to get there.

    We set entrance questions but don’t panic this is not a test. It’s to show you the level we will start working at. If you answer the majority of these confidently then you will be comfortable working at our level.

    Enrol now and we will contact you during August with full details of our entrance questions.

    TIME, DATE, AND PLACE?

    Every Saturday in term-time at UCL – near to Euston Station.

    MathsCircles are typically two hours in the afternoons and MathsBattles are usually twice a term and can be whole-day events.

    Our 2018-19 sessions start in early October; full details will be confirmed nearer the time.

    This Maths Club is free of charge (sponsored by UCL and WeSolveProblems)
