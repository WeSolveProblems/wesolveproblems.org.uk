Problem ID 60437

Each side in the triangle ABC is divided into 8 equal segments. How many different triangles exist with the vertices at the points of division $($the points A, B, C cannot be the vertices of triangles$)$ in which neither side is parallel to either side of the triangle ABC?

Solution

Let $N_a$ be the number of triangles in which one side is parallel to the side BC of the original triangle. Similarly, we define the numbers $N_b, N_c, N_{a, b}, N_{b, c}, N_{a, c}$ and $N_{a, b, c}$. Let N denote the total number of triangles. Then $N = 7^3, N_a = N_b = N_c = 7^2, N_{a, b} = N_{b, c} = N_{a, c} = 7, N_{a, b, c} = 1$.
