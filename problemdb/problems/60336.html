Problem ID 60336

How many different seven-digit numbers exist $($it is assumed that the number cannot start with zero$)$?

Solution

Solution 1

The first digit can be selected in 9 ways, and each of the other six - in 10 ways. In total, we have $9 \times 10^6$ ways.

Solution 2

There are as many such numbers as there are seven-digit numbers. The largest seven-digit number is 9999999 and the largest six-digit number is 999999. Therefore, in total there are 9999999 - 999999 = 9000000 seven-digit numbers.

Answer

$9 \times 10^6$ numbers.
