Problem ID 60389

How many rational terms are contained in the expansion of

a) $(\sqrt 2 + \sqrt{4}{3} )^100$;

b) $(\sqrt 2 + \sqrt{3}{3} )^300$?

Solution

a) For the number to be rational, k must be a multiple of 4. There are 26 such integers: 0, 4, 8, ..., 96, 100.

b) For the number to be rational, k must be a multiple of 3, and 300 - k must be even. Hence, k is a multiple of 6. Among the integers from 0 to 300 there are $300 \div 6 + 1 = 51$ such numbers.

Answer

a) 26; b) 51.
