Problem ID 58299

Suppose that n $ \geq $ 3. Are there n points that do not lie on one line, whose pairwise distances are irrational, and the areas of all of the triangles with vertices in them are rational?

Solution

Yes, there are. Consider the points $P_i = (i, i^2)$, where i = 1, ..., n. The areas of all of the triangles with vertices at the nodes of the integer lattice are rational $($see problem 24.5$)$, and the numbers $P_iP_j = | i - j | \sqrt {1+ (i + j)^2} $ are irrational.

Answer

Yes, there are.
