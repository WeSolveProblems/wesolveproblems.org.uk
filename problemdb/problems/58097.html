Problem ID 58097

Several circles, whose total length of circumferences is 10, are placed inside a square of side 1. Prove that there will always be some straight line that crosses at least four of the circles.

Solution

Project all of the given circles onto the side $AB$ of the square $ABCD$. The projection of a circle of circumference $l$ is a line segment of length $ \frac {l}{\pi}$ - the diameter of the circle. Therefore the sum of all of the projections, or line segments, of the circles is equal to $ \frac {10}{\pi} > 3$.

Therefore the line $AB$ contains a point which lies on the projection of at least 4 circles. Therefore a perpendicular to $AB$ drawn through this point will cross at least 4 circles, as required.
