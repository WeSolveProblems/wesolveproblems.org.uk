Problem ID 104046

A cannibal has 25 prisoners in a cellar:

a) In how many ways can he choose three of them for breakfast, lunch and dinner? Note that the order is important.

b) And how many ways are there to choose three to set free?

Solution

a) For breakfast, the cannibal can choose any of the 25 people, for lunch - any of the remaining 24, and for dinner - one of the remaining 23 people. In total, we get $25 \times 24 \times 23$ = 13 800 ways.

b) Note that in the previous part, each group of three prisoners was counted $3 \times 2 \times 1$ = 6 times. Since now their order is not important to us, the answer is 13800/6 = 2300.

Answer

a) 13 800 ways; b) 2 300 ways.
