Problem ID 88141

Write a number instead of the space $($in letters, not numbers!$)$ to get a true sentence:

THIS SENTENCE HAS ... LETTERS

$($Note that the dash does not count as a letter, i.e. the word twenty-two is made up of 9 letters$)$.

Hint

Note: now there are twenty-two letters in the sentence.

Solution

Now there are twenty-two letters in the sentence. This means that we must insert a number of at least 22, which will add at least 9 letters. But if we insert numbers 28 or 29, the sentence does not become true. Hence, the required number is not less than 30, which adds, at least, the 6 letters. After going over all the numbers from 31 to 39, we get two possible answers "This sentence has thirty-one letters" or “This sentence has thirty-three letters”.

Answer

"This sentence has thirty-one letters" or “This sentence has thirty-three letters”.
