Problem ID 88164

Colin always tells the truth, but Dave always lies. What question should they be asked, so that they would give the same answers?

Hint

Remember problem number 140.

Solution

As in Problem 140, the question should relate to a characteristic that is true of one of the boys $($for example, Colin$)$ and is incorrect for the other. There are 2 characteristics, according to the conditions of the problem: name and honesty. Therefore, there can be two questions in the simplest form: "Do you always tell the truth?" or: "Are you called Colin?" If you want, you can complicate any of these questions indefinitely, for example: "Does your friend tell the truth?" or "Is your friend's name Colin?" or "Are you more honest than your friend?" etc. It is interesting to compare the questions: "Are you more honest than Dave?" and "Are you more honest than Colin?". To the first question, both of the boys will respond identically - "yes", and to the second - differently, for Colin cannot be more honest than himself and, like an honest person, he recognises this.

Answer

"Is your name Colin?"
