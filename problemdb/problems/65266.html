Problem ID 65266

On each of four cards there is written a natural number. Take two cards at random and add the numbers on them. With equal probability, this amount can be less than 9, equal to 9 or more 9. What numbers can be written on the cards?

Solution

Let the natural numbers a $\geq$ b $\geq$ c $\geq$ d be written on four cards. Two of the four cards can be selected in six different ways. Since by assumption a pair of cards are randomly selected, the probability of choosing a specific pair is 1/6. Since by the condition of the event {s $<$ 9}, {s = 9}, {s $>$ 9} $($where s is the sum of the numbers on the selected cards$)$ are equally probable, then the probability of each of them is 1/3.

Hence, two of the sums a + b, a + c, a + d, b + c, b + d, c + d are 9, two are greater than 9 and two are less. By virtue of the inequalities a + b $\geq$ a + c $\geq$ a + d $\geq$ b + d $\geq$ c + d and a + c $\geq$ b + c $\geq$ b + d, the sums a + d and b + c are equal to 9, less than 9 are the sums a + b and a + c, and more than 9 - the sums b + d and c + d. In particular, a + c $<$ a + d, hence c $<$ d. Similarly, a $<$ b. In addition, b $<$ c, since the sum b + c is odd.
