Problem ID 57304

Prove that $($a + b - c$)$/2 $<$ m_c $<$ $($a + b$)$/2, where a, b and c are the lengths of the sides of an arbitrary triangle and $m_c$ is the median to side c.

Solution

Let $C_1$ be the midpoint of AB. Then $CC_1$ + $C_1A$ $>$ CA and $BC_1$ + $C_1C$ $>$ BC. Therefore, $2CC_1$ + BA $>$ CA + BC, that is, $m_c$ $>$ $($a + b - c$)$/2. Let the point C' be symmetric to C with respect to $C_1$. Then $CC_1$ = $C-1Câ€™$ and BC' = CA. Therefore, $2m_c$ = CC' $<$ CB + BC' = CB + CA, that is, $m_c$ $<$ $($a + b$)$/2.
