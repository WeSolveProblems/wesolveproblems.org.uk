Problem ID 61013

Prove that if $(p, q) = 1$ and p/q is a rational root of the polynomial $P (x) = a_nx^n + ... + a_1x + a_0$ with integer coefficients, then

a) $a_0$ is divisible by p;

b) $a_n$ is divisible by q.

Solution

The condition $f (p/q) = 0$ can be written in the form $a_0q^n + a_1pq^{n-1} + ... + a_np^n = 0$. All of the terms on the left-hand side are multiples of p except for the first, hence $a_0q^n$ is divisible by p. But p and q are relatively prime, hence $a_0$ is divisible by p. Similarly, we prove that $a_n$ is a multiple of q.

See the solution above.

Remarks

These relations allow us to list all of the rational numbers that can be the roots of a given polynomial.
