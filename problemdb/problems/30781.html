Problem ID 30781

Prove that there exists a graph with 2n vertices whose degrees are 1, 1, 2, 2, ..., n, n.

Hint

Use induction on n.

Solution

We will construct the graph by induction. Base case. For n = 1, such a graph exists: two vertices connected by an edge. Inductive step. Suppose that a graph with 2n vertices and the indicated powers is already constructed. Add two vertices A and B to it. We join the vertex A with n old vertices with powers of 1, ..., n, and also with the vertex B. As a result, the degrees of half of the old vertices do not change, whilst the remaining ones will increase by 1, the degree of A will be equal to n + 1, and the degree of B will be 1. This is the required graph with 2n + 2 vertices.
