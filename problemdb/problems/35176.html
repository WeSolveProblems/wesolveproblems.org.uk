Problem ID 31576

Find the number of zeros in which the number 11100 - 1 ends.

Hint

Use the binomial expansion of the expression $(10 + 1)^100$.

Solution

S = $(10 + 1)^100$ - 1 = ... + $(100 \times 99 \times 98/ 6)$ \times $10^3$ + $(100 \times 99/ 2)$ \times $10^2$ + $100 \times 10$ + 1 = ... + $(33 \times 49)$ \times $10^5$ + 495000 + 1000 = A + 496000, where all the numbers in the place of the ellipses are divisible by $10^4$, that is, A ends in at least four zero. Hence it follows that the number S ends in three zeros $($ and the fourth digit of the number S is equal to 6 $)$.

Answer

Three zeros.
