Problem ID 102841

Two weighings. There are 7 coins which are identical on the surface, including 5 real ones $($all of the same weight$)$ and 2 counterfeit coins $($both of the same weight, but lighter than the real ones$)$. How can you find the 3 real coins with the help of two weighings on scales without weights?

Solution

We number the coins 1, 2, ..., 7. For the first weighing, weigh the coins 1, 2, 3 on one side, and on the other side, the coins 4, 5, 6.

a) In the case when the scales are in equilibrium, there is one false coin on each side, and therefore, 7 is a real coin. The second weighing is made up of 1 and 2 coins. When the scales are in equilibrium then the coins 1, 2, 7 are real. If 1 is heavier than 2, then 1, 3, 7 are real.

b) If there is no equilibrium during the first weighing, the heavier group of three coins consists of real coins.
