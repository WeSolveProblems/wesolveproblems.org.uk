Problem ID 66057

According to one implausible legend, Cauchy and Bunyakovsky were very fond of playing darts in the evenings. But the target was unusual - the sectors on it were unequal, so the probability of getting into different sectors was not the same. Once Cauchy throws a dart and hits the target. Bunyakovsky throws the next one. Which is more likely: that Bunyakovsky will hit the same sector that Cauchy’s dart went into, or that his dart will land on the next sector clockwise?

Solution

Let probabilities of hitting the sectors be equal to $p_1, p_2, ..., p_n$ $($indices indicate the sector numbers$)$. The probability that Bunyakovsky will hit the same sector as Cauchy is equal to $p_1^2 + p_2^2 + ... + p_n^2$ , and the probability that Bunyakovsky will hit the next clockwise sector is equal to $p_1p_2 + p_2p_3 + ... + p_{n-1}p_n + p_np_1$.

It obviously follows from the inequality $a^2 + b^2 ≥ 2ab$ that

In this case, equality is attained only in the case when $p_1 = p_2 = ... = p_n$, which contradicts the condition.

Answer

That he will hit the same sector.

Remarks

5 marks.
