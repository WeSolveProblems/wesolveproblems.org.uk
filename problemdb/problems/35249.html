Problem ID 35249

On a plane, there are given 2004 points. The distances between every pair of points is noted. Prove that among these noted distances at least thirty numbers are different.

Hint

In the opposite case, all points except one are located on 29 circles.

Solution

DSuppose that there is a counterexample. We choose one of these points - A. Since the distance from point A to any other point takes less than 30 different values, the remaining 2003 points lie on 29 circles with centre A. Since $69\times29$ = 2001 which is less than 2003, no less than 70 points are located on one of these circles. We consider only points on this circle $($let's call it S$)$ and choose one of the points - B. The remaining 69 points are located on 29 circles with the centre B. However, each of these circles has, at most, two common points with the circle S, and $2 \times 29$ $<$ 69. Therefore, this is a contradiction.
