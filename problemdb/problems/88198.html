Problem ID 88198

What are the eight coins you need to take, so that you can use them to pay without change any amount from 1 pence up to 1 pound? $($In circulation at the time, there were coins of 1, 3, 5, 10, 20 and 50 pence$)$.

Hint

Try to break this problem into two: first find coins with which you can pay any amount from 1 to 10 pence, then â€“ coins with which you can pay 10, 20, ..., 90 pence.

Solution

Take coins worth 1, 1, 3, 5, 10, 10, 20, 50 pence. It is easy to verify that the first four coins allow you to pay any amount from 1 to 9 pence, and the last four - any multiple of 10 from 10 to 100 pence. Therefore, having this set of coins, you can pay any amount from 1 pence up to 100 pence.

Answer

1, 1, 3, 5, 10, 10, 20, 50 pence.
