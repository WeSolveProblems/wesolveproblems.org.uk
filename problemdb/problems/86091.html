Problem ID 86091

In the numbers of MEXAILO and LOMONOSOV, each letter denotes a number $($different letters correspond to different numbers$)$. It is known that the products of the numbers of these two words are equal. Can both numbers be odd?

Solution

Note that 10 different letters are used, therefore each digit is indicated by a letter, in particular, among these numbers there is zero. Thus, the product of the digits of one number $($and hence of the second$)$ is zero. Consequently, both of these numbers have a zero. In the words of MEXAILO and LOMONOSOV, the common letters are M, L and O, therefore zero is denoted by one of them. This cannot be L and M, since the numbers cannot start with a zero. Hence, zero is denoted by the letter O. In the number of MEXAILO the zero is at the end, that is, this number is even.

Answer

No.
