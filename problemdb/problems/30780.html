Problem ID 30780

Prove that there is no graph with five vertices whose degrees are equal to 4, 4, 4, 4, 2.

Solution

Let the degree of each of the four vertices be equal to 4. This means that each of them is connected to all of the other vertices $($including the fifth one$)$. Hence, the degree of the fifth vertex is also equal to 4.
