Problem ID 111415

The volume of the regular quadrangular pyramid SABCD is equal to V. The height SP of the pyramid is the edge of the regular tetrahedron SPQR, the plane of the face PQR which is perpendicular to the edge SC. Find the volume of the common part of these pyramids.

Solution

We denote $AB = a, \angle SCP = α$. Let the plane passing through the line BD be perpendicular to the edge SC of the pyramid SABCD, intersecting this edge at the point H. The height SP of the pyramid SABCD is the edge of the regular tetrahedron SPQR, and the height of the tetrahedron lies on the line SC. Therefore, the plane of the face of the PQR of the tetrahedron passes through the point P perpendicular to SC, and hence coincides with the plane BDH.
