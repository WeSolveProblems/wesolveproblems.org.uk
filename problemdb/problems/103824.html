Problem ID 103824

A globe has 17 parallels and 24 meridians. How many parts is the globeâ€™s surface divided into? The meridian is an arc connecting the North Pole with the South Pole. A parallel is a circle parallel to the equator $($the equator is also a parallel$)$.

Hint

Solve the problem for two meridians and one parallel.

Solution

Meridians divide the globe into 24 parts $($slices$)$, and the parallels divide each slice into 17 + 1 = 18 pieces. Therefore, we have $18 \times 24$ = 432 parts in total.

Answer

Into 432 parts.
