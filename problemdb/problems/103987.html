Problem

a) Prove that in any football team there are two players who were born on the same day of the week.

b) Prove that in the population of London, which is almost 9 million, there will be ten thousand people who celebrate their birthday on the same day.

Solution

a) Picture the football players as pigeons, and the days of the week as roosting holes. We have 7 roosting holes in which we need to place 11 pigeons. Using the Pigeon Hole Principle, there will be at least one hole containing at least two pigeons.

b) Consider that on each of the 366 days of the year - including leap years - fewer than 10,000 Londoners are born. This would imply that in the entirety of London there are no more than $366 \times 9 999 = 3 659 634$ people living in London, which as the actual population of London is almost 9 Million must be false.
