Problem

Every point in a plane, which has whole number co-ordinates is plotted in one of $n$ colours. Prove that there will be a rectangle made out of 4 points of the same colour.

Hint

Consider the whole-number points on an infinitely long horizontal strip in the plane. There will be two columns on the strip, where the order of colours of points is the same.

Solution

Consider a strip consisting of $n+1$ consecutive horizontal rows of points. Consider the vertical columns of this strip, consisting of $n+1$ points. There is only a finite number of ways of plotting $n+1$ points in $n$ colours (the first point can be plotted $n$ different ways. Indepndently of this the second point can also be plotted in $n$ different ways, and so on. In total there are $n^(n+1)$ was of plotting $n+1$ points in $n$ different colours).

Amongst the vertical columns of the strip being considered (of which there are infinitely many) there will be two identically plotted columns $A$ and $B$, that is the points in these two columns located in the $i$th row, where $i=1, 2,...,n+1$ are plotted in the same colour.

Since column $A$ contains $n+1$ whole-numbered points, there will be two points in this column - $X$ and $Y$ which have the same colour. In row $B$ the points $X'$ and $Y'$ are located in the same rows as $X$ and $Y$ and plotted in the same colour as $X$ and $Y$ respectively (since $A$ and $B$ are plotted in the same colours).

Therefore the points $X, Y, X', Y'$ act as the vertices of a rectangle, and have the same colour - so are the 4 points being searched for.
