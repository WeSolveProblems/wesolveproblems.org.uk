Problem ID 103883

On an island, there are knights who always tell the truth, and liars who always lie. The traveller met three of the islanders and asked each of them: '' How many knights are there among your companions? ''. The first one answered: "None." The second one said: '' One ''. What did the third man say?

Solution

If the first is a knight, then by virtue of his words, the second and third are liars, which is impossible because of the statement of the second islander. So, the first one is a liar. If the second is a liar, then by his words the third is also a liar, but then the first one told the truth, and he had to lie. So, the second one is a knight. By virtue of his words, the third is also a knight. The third islander will answer honestly: '' One ''.

Answer

The third one said '' One ''.
