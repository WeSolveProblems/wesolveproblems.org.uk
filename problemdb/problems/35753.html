Problem ID 35753

A convex polygon on a plane contains no fewer than $m^2+1$ points with whole number co-ordinates. Prove that within the polygon there are $m+1$ points with whole number co-ordinates that lie on a single straight line.

Hint

Colour in the points in the plane in $m^2$ different colours. There will be two points of the same colour.

Solution

By the pigeon hole principle, within $m^2+1$ points with whole-number co-ordinates there will be two points $(k, l)$ and $k_1, l_1$ such that $k=k_1$ and $l=l_1$ - mod $m$.

Therefore there will be $m+1$ points $ \left ( k+ \frac{k_1-k}{m}i , l+ \frac{l_1-l}{m}i \right ) , 0 \leq i \leq m, $ that have whole number co-ordinates and lie on the line segment connecting $(k, l)$ and $k_1, l_1$.
