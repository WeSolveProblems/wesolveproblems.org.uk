Problem ID 65337

Peter and 9 other people play such a game: everyone rolls a dice. The player receives a prize if he or she rolled a number that no one else was able to roll.

a) What is the probability that Peter will receive a prize?

b) What is the probability that at least someone will receive a prize?

Solution

a) Suppose $($for the sake of clarity only$)$ that Peter throws first. He rolled a certain number of points. The probability that each next player will roll another number of points is 5/6. Since the rolls are independent, the probability that the nine remaining players will not roll the same number as Peter is equal to $(5/6)^9 ≈ 0.194$.

b) Let’s number the players and consider the events $A_j$ = {the j-th player will receive the prize}, where j = 1, 2, ..., 10. From a) it is clear that $P (A_j) = (5/6)^9$.
