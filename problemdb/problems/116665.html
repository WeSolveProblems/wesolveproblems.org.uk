Problem ID 116665

Four children said the following about each other.
Mary: Sarah, Nathan and George solved the problem.
Sarah: Mary, Nathan and George didn't solve the problem.
Nathan: Mary and Sarah lied.
George: Mary, Sarah and Nathan told the truth.
How many of the children actually told the truth?

Solution

Mary's and Sarah's statements contradict each other, and it follows that George definitely lied. Then we have two possible cases.
1) Nathan told the truth. Then Mary and Sarah also lied, so then only one child told the truth, i.e. Nathan.
2) Nathan lied. Then either Mary or Sarah told the truth. Then in this case we also have that only one child told the truth.

Answer

One child.
