Problem ID 30682

Let p be a prime number, and a is not divisible by p. Prove that there is a positive integer b such that ab $\equiv$ 1 $($mod p$)$.

Solution 1

$a \times a^p$ - 2= $a^p$ - 1 $\equiv$1 $($mod p$)$

Solution 2

The numbers 0, a, 2a, ..., $($p - 1$)$a yield different remainders when divided by p: ia - ja = $($i - j$)$a is not divisible by p, since $|i - j|$$<$p. Consequently, these remainders take all possible values, including 1.
