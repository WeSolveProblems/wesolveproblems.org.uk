Problem ID 35734

There are 20 students in a class, and each one is friends with at least 14 others. Can you prove that there are four students in this class who are all friends?

Hint

One of the students has 14 friends. It is sufficient to choose from these friends three pair-wise acquaintances.

Solution

We will put the whole class in one room. Let's consider some person A. Now let out of the room all the students who are not friends with A. By the condition, of such there are no more than five. Therefore, at least 15 students remain in the room. Choose from the remaining students in the room, a student B, different from A. Let out of the room all the students who are not friends with B. After that, there were at least 10 students left in the room. Finally, we choose from the remaining students in the room, a student C, different from A and B. Let out of the room all the students who are not friends with C. After that, there are at least five students left in the room. These five friends are A, B, C and two other students D and E who are friends with A, B, C. The four students who you are looking for are A, B, C, D.

Answer

Yes you can.
