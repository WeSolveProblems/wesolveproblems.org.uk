Problem ID 87943

There are two hourglasses - one for 7 minutes and another for 11 minutes. An egg is boiled for 15 minutes. How can this time be measured with the help of the available hourglasses?

Hint

With the help of two different hourglasses, you can measure not only the time equal to their "sum", but also the time equal to their "difference".

Solution

"Turn on" at the same time the two hourglasses. When the 7-minute hourglass is finished, we turn it over and let both run for 4 minutes, until the end of the 11-minute hourglass. Now we turn back over the 7-minute hourglass, which will have been seeping for exactly 4 minutes, and time until the whole hourglass was "poured" out. This whole process would then add up to 15 minutes.
