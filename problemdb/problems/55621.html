Problem ID 55621

A rectangular billiard with sides 1 and $ \sqrt {2} $ is given. From its angle at an angle of 45 Â° to the side a ball is released. Will it ever get into one of the pockets? $($The pockets are in the corners of the billiard table$)$.

Hint

With each collision of the ball against the wall, we will reflect the billiard table relative to this wall.

Solution

With each collision of the ball with the wall, we will reflect the billiard table relative to this wall. Thus, the ball will always move along a straight line along the "chequered paper", the cells of which are billiard tables, and will never get into the node of the cell, $($since 1 and $ \sqrt {2} $ are incommensurable$)$, i.e. it will never fall into one of the pockets.

Answer

No, it will not.
