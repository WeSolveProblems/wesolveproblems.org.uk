Problem ID 30689

How many ways can Susan choose 4 colours from 7 different ones?

Solution

Solution 1

If we follow the reasoning as in problem number 30687, we find that the number of these methods $($$)$ is $\frac{7 \times 6 \times 5 \times4}{4!}$.

Solution 2

If we choose 4 colours, then there will be 3 left. That means the required number is equal to the number of ways to choose three colours from seven, that is $\frac{7 \times 6 \times 5}{3!}$.

Answer

35 ways.
