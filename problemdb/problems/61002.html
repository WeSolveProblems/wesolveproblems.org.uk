Problem ID 61002

Prove that any polynomial P $(x)$ of degree n can be uniquely expanded in powers of x-c: P $(x)$ = $c_k$ $(x - c)^k$, and the coefficients $c_k$ can be found from $c_k$ = $(0 \geq k \geq n)$.

Hint

The existence of an expansion is proved by the multiple division by x-c with a remainder. Uniqueness follows from the above formula for the coefficients, which is verified by direct differentiation.
