Problem ID 34862

There are 30 ministers in a parliament. Each two of them are either friends or enemies, and each is friends with exactly six others. Every three ministers form a committee. Find the total number of committees in which all three members are friends or all three are enemies.

Hint

How many committees is a minister a member of? In how many committees are the remaining two members the friend and the enemy of the minister?

Solution

We denote the ministers by dots. We connect the dots with a red line if the respective ministers are friends and with a blue line if they are enemies. We need to find the number of single-coloured triangles with vertices at these points. The number of all triangles is equal to .

Let's first count the number of non-coloured triangles. In each such triangle, there are exactly two angles in which the red and blue segments converge $($let's call such angles multi-coloured$)$. In single-coloured triangles there are no multi-coloured corners. In each vertex, by hypothesis, 6 red and 23 blue segments converge, that is, there are $6 \times 23$ = 138 multi-coloured corners with a fixed vertex. The total number of multi-coloured triangles, therefore, is $138 \times 30$/2 = 2070. And the number of single-colour triangles is equal to 4060 - 2070 = 1990.

Answer

1990 committees.
