Problem ID 79391

At what value of K is the quantity $A_k$ = $(19^k + 66^k)$/k! at its maximum? You are given a number x that is greater than 1. Is the following inequality necessarily fulfilled [$\sqrt[\sqrt{x}$]] = [$\sqrt{\sqrt{x}}$]?

Solution

Let a be a natural number such that $a^4$ $≤$ x $<$ $(a + 1)^4$. Then $a^2$ $≤$ $\sqrt{x}$ $<$ $(a + 1)^2$. Hence, on the one hand, a $≤$ $\sqrt{\sqrt{x}$ $<$ a + 1, [$\sqrt{\sqrt{x}$] = a. On the other hand, $a^2$ $≤$ [$\sqrt{x}$] $<$ $(a + 1)^2$, a $\geq$ $\sqrt[\sqrt{x}]$ $<$ a + 1, [$sqrt[\sqrt{x}]$] = a.

Answer

Yes, for all x
