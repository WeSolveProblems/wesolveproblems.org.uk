Problem ID 98031

10 friends sent one another greetings cards; each sent 5 cards. Prove that there will be two friends who sent cards to one another.

Solution

Solution 1

Consider that the statement is false, that is there is no pair of friends that sent one another cards. Each of the friends sent 5 cards, therefore they could receive a maximum of 4 cards each as neither they, nor the 5 friends they sent a card to, can send them a card back, leaving 4 possible friends. This means that maximum total number of cards received is 40. This is despite there being $5 \times 10 =50$ cards sent. This is a contradiction.

Solution 2

The number of pairs of friends $=\frac{10 \times 9}{2}=45$ Since in total 50 cards were sent there must be some pairs with at least two cards sent. That is in that pair the friends sent cards to one another.

Notes

1. From solution 2 it follows that the number of pairs of friends who sent cards to one another is no less than 5 - no friend sends a card to the same person more than once.
