Problem ID 60869

Prove that there is at most one point of an integer lattice on a circle with centre at $(\sqrt 2, \sqrt 3)$.

Solution

Suppose that two points $(x; y)$ and $(u; v)$ lie on the circle. Then $(x - \sqrt {2})^2 + (v - \sqrt {3})^2 = (u - \sqrt {2})^2 + (v - \sqrt {3})^2$,

$2 (u - x) \sqrt {2} + 2 (v - y) \sqrt {3} = u^2 + v^2 - x^2 - y^2$.

The impossibility of the last equality is proved by squaring.
