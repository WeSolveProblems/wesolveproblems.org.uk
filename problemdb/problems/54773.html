Problem ID 54773

Ten straight lines are drawn through a point on a plane, after which the plane is cut along these straight lines into angles. Prove that at least one of these angles is less than 20$^{\circ}$.

Hint

Suppose that each of the angles obtained is not less than 20$^{\circ}$.

Solution

Ten straight lines, drawn through one point, split the plane into 20 angles. If they are all not less than 20$^{\circ}$, then their sum is not less than $20 \times 20$ $^{\circ}$ = 400$^{\circ}$ which is greater than 360$^{\circ}$. This is a contradiction.
