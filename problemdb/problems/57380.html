Problem ID 57380

Prove that if the angles of a convex pentagon form an arithmetic progression, then each of them is greater than 36$^{\circ}$.

Solution

Let the angles of the pentagon be equal to $\alpha$, $\alpha$ + $\gamma$, $\alpha$ + 2 $\gamma$, $\alpha$ + 3 $\gamma$, $\alpha$ + 4 $\gamma$, where $\alpha$, $\gamma$ $\geq$ 0. Since the sum of the angles of the pentagon is 3 $\pi$, then 5 $\alpha$ + 10 $\gamma$ = 3 $\pi$. It follows from the convexity of a pentagon that all its angles are less than $\pi$, that is, $\alpha$ + 4 $\gamma$ $<$ $\pi$, or -5 $\alpha$ / 2 - 10 $\gamma$ > - 5 $\pi$ / 2. Adding the last inequality with the equality 5 $\alpha$ + 10 $\gamma$ = 3 $\pi$, we obtain 5 $\alpha$ /2 $>$ $\pi$ / 2, that is, $\alpha$ $>$ $\pi$ / 5 = 36$^{\circ}$.
