Problem ID 32056

Each of the four gnomes - Dopey, Sleepy, Happy, Moody - either always speaks the truth, or always lies. We heard such a conversation: Dopey to Sleepy: "you are a liar"; Happy to Sleepy: "you are a liar"; Moody to Happy: "Yes, they are both liars," $($after a moment's thought$)$, "however, so are you." Which of them is telling the truth?

Solution

Suppose that Moody is telling the truth. Then, according to him, the three other gnomes are liars. And, thus, Happy's statement is true. Hence, the assumption leads to a contradiction, so Moody is a liar, and his assertion that Happy is a liar is false. Hence we conclude that Happy is telling the truth. Thus, Dopey is a liar, and Sleepy speaks the truth. Note that the phrase "Yes and they are both liars" $($regarding to Sleepy and Dopey$)$ is false $($despite the fact that Dopey is really a liar$)$, since Sleepy is not a liar.

Remarks

Source of the solution: the book "VO.Bugayenko., Lomonosov Tournaments., Competitions in Mathematics., MCNMO-CheRo, 1998".
