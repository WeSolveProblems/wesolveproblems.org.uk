Problem ID 116474

Four numbers (from 1 to 9) have been used to create two numbers with four-digits each. These two numbers are the maximum and minimum numbers, respectively, possible. The sum of these two numbers is equal to 11990. What could the two numbers be?

Solution

Let us call the four numbers which make up the other two be $A$, $B$, $C$ and $D$ in increasing order. Then the smallest four digit number made up of $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$ is $ABCD$ and the biggest is $DCBA$. Thus we get the rebus $ABCD + DCBA = 11990$. (Note that different letters could stand for the same number.) It immediately follows that $A + D = 10$. Therefore a 1 would carry from the hundreds digit to the thousands digit (in the sum), which means that $B+C>9$. Then from the tens digit we can see that $B+C=18$. Then $B=C=9$. It follows that $D=9$ and $A=1$.

Answer

9991 and 1999.
