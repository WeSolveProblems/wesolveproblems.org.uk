Problem ID 86553

A game with 25 coins. In a row there are 25 coins. For a turn it is allowed to take one or two neighbouring coins. The player who has nothing to take loses.

Solution

The first takes the middle 13th coin. If the second takes the kth coins, then the first takes the 26-k-th coin; if the second takes two coins, then the first takes two coins according to the previously written rule and wins $($ the symmetry of moves relative to the middle coin $)$.
