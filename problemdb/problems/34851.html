Problem 34851

15 MPs take part in a debate. During the debate, each one criticises exactly $k$ of the 14 other contributors. For what minimum value of $k$ is it possible to definitively state that there will be two MPs who have criticised one another?

Hint

Consider a diagram where the vertices of a polygon represent an MP, and a line between $A$ and $B$ indicates that MP $A$ criticised MP $B$.

Solution

If each MP criticises 8 others, then the number of lines drawn on the diagram suggested in the hint would be $15 \times 8 = 120$ which is greater than the number of pairs of vertices $^{15}C_{2} = 105$. This means that some pair of vertices have at least two lines linking them, that is there are two MPs who criticised one another.

On the other hand, let 15 MPs be sitting around a round table. Each one criticises the next 7 people sitting next to them in a clockwise direction. It is clear to see that in this situation no two MPs criticise one another.

Answer

When $k=8$
