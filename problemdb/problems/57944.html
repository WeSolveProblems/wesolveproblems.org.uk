Problem ID 57944

Prove that if you rotate through an angle of $\alpha$ with the center at the origin, the point with the coordinates $($x, y$)$, it goes to the point $(x cos \alpha - y sin \alpha, x sin \alpha + y cos \alpha)$.

Solution

If the point X = $($x, y$)$ is located at a distance R from the origin O and the OX beam forms an angle $\varphi$ with the $O_x$ axis, then x = R cos $\varphi$, y = R sin $\varphi$. Therefore, if we rotate through an angle of $\alpha$, the point X goes to a point with coordinates

x’ = R cos $(\alpha)$ = R cos $\varphi cos \alpha - R sin \varphi sin \alpha$ = x cos $\alpha - y sin \alpha$,

y’ = R sin $(\alpha)$ = R sin $\alpha + R cos \varphi sin \alpha$ = x sin $\alpha + y cos \alpha$.
