Problem ID 65056

Author: I.S. Rubanov

On the table, there are 7 cards with numbers from 0 to 6. Two take turns in taking one card. The winner is the one is the first person who can, from his cards, make up a natural number that is divisible by 17. Who will win in a regular game the person who goes first or second?

Solution

Denote the players A (the player who goes first) and B (the player who goes second). Let's give a strategy that allows A to win. Let him take the number 3 in the first round; then B is forced to take 4 (otherwise A will take it in the second turn and win, making the number 34). Note that then B will not win with her second move, because the only two-digit number containing 4 in its record and dividing by 17 is 34. Next, A takes 1, threatening to make the next number 51 or the number 136. As shown above, B immediately cannot win. Let’s look at these two threats simultaneously, B also cannot win and therefore, A’s next move will be the winning one.

Answer

The first player.

Remarks

There are other winning strategies for A.
