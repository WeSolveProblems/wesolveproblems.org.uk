Problem ID 88336

Decipher the numerical puzzle system

MA x MA = MIR

AM x AM = RIM

$($different letters correspond to different numbers, and identical letters correspond to the same numbers$)$.

Solution

From the first equation, it follows that M = 1, and from the second, that A does not exceed 3, then A = 2 or A = 3. Since $12 \times$ 12 = 144, then we get from the first equation that I = R, which is a contradiction. The case A = 3 gives the answer: $13 \times 13$ = 169, $31 \times 31$ = 961.
