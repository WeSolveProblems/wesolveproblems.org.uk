Problem ID 35501

Arrows are placed on the sides of a polygon. Prove that the number of vertices in which two arrows converge is equal to the number of vertices from which two arrows emerge.

Hint

The number of emerging arrows is equal to the number of vertices of the polygon.

Solution

Let n be the number of sides of the given polygon, and let k be the number of vertices where two arrows converge. In total, the number of arrows is n, of which 2k arrows converge at the given k vertices, and the remaining n - 2k arrows converge at the n - 2k vertices $($one in each$)$. Now, n - k - $($n - 2k$)$ = k vertices remain, where no arrows converge, that is, where two arrows emerge.
