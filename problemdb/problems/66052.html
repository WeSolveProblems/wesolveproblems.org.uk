Problem ID 66052

A sequence consists of 19 ones and 49 zeros, arranged in a random order. We call the maximal subsequence of the same symbols a "group". For example, in the sequence 110001001111 there are five groups: two ones, then three zeros, then one one, then two zeros and finally four ones. Find the mathematical expectation of the length of the first group.

Solution

Separately, we number the ones by the numbers from 1 to 19 and separately, the zeros by numbers from 1 to 49. Let $I_k$ be the indicator of the event "there are no zeros before the k-th unit" and let $J_m$ be the indicator of the event "there are no ones before m-th zero", where $1 ≤ k ≤ 19$ and $1 ≤ m ≤ 19$. If at least one of the indicators $I_k$ is equal to one, then the first group consists of ones, and therefore all of the indicators $J_m$ are equal to zero. Conversely, if at least one of the indicators $J_m$ is equal to one, then all of the $I_k$ are equal to zero. The required quantity X "length of the first group" is equal to $I_1 + I_2 + ... + I_{19} + J_1 + J_2 + ... + J_{49}$.

The probability of the event "$I_k$ = 1" is equal to 1/50, since this event occurs only when, in a randomly intermixed sequence consisting of the k-th one and all 49 zeros, the one is first. Therefore, $EI_k = P (I_k = 1) = 0.02$. Similarly, $EJ_m = P (J_m = 1) = 1/20 = 0.05$. Therefore $EX = 19 \times 0.02 + 49 \times 0.05 = 2.823$.

Answer

2.83.
