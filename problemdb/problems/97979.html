Problem ID 97979

Prove that in any group of 7 natural numbers - not necessarily consecutive - it is possible to choose three numbers such that their sum is divisible by 3.

Solution

By the pigeon hole principle it is possible to choose three numbers out of the 7 that have the same denominator when divided by 3. Therefore their sum is also divisible by 3.

Notes

1. It is not difficult to see that this is possible even for five natural numbers.
