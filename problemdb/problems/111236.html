Problem ID 111236

There is a kingdom inhabited by liars and knights. The knights always tell the truth and the liars always lie. The knights always carry a sword with them but the liars do not. Two knights and two liars met and looked at each other. Which of them could say the phrase:
1) "All of us are knights."
2) "Only one of you is a knight."
3) "Exactly two of you are knights."

Solution

1) A knight could not have said this, since this is not true. This means that any of the liars could have said that.
2) Any of the knights could have said this since it would true for them. Any of the liars could also have said that, since it would be a lie for them.
3) Nobody could have said this since for any knight this statement would be false, and for any liar tis statement would be true.
