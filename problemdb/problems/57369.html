Problem ID 57369

In the trapezoid ABCD, the angles at the base AD satisfy the inequalities $\angle$A $<$ $\angle$D $<$ 90$^{\circ}$. Prove that AC $>$ BD.

Solution

Let $B_1$ and $C_1$ be the projections of the points B and C onto the base AD. Since $\angle$$BAB_1$ $<$ $\angle$$CDC_1$ and $BB_1$ = $CC_1$, then $AB_1$ $>$ $DC_1$ and therefore $B_1D$ $<$ $AC_1$. Therefore, $BD^2$ = $B_1D^2 + B_1B^2 < AC_1^2 + CC_1^2 = AC^2$.
