Problem ID 103002

In a room there are 85 balloons - red and blue ones. It is known that: 1$)$ at least one of the balls is red, 2$)$ from each arbitrarily chosen pair of balls at least one is blue. How many red balls are there in the room?

Hint

Think about whether there can be two red balls in the room.

Solution

Since one of any two balls is blue, there can not be two red balls in the room. So, there 84 blue balloons in the room and 1 red balloon.
