Problem ID 35627

A hostess bakes a cake for some guests. Either 10 or 11 people can come to her house. What is the smallest number of pieces she needs to cut the cake into $($in advance$)$ so that it can be divided equally between 10 and 11 guests?

Hint

If 10 guests come, then everyone should get at least two pieces.

Solution

If 10 guests come, then everyone should get at least two pieces. Otherwise one of the 10 guests would get one piece which is 1/10 of the cake, and if 11 guests come, then this piece would have to be divided further. Thus, the number of pieces is not less than $2 \times 10$ = 20. We will show twenty pieces are enough. Letâ€™s cut the cake into 10 pieces of 1/11 of the cake and 10 pieces of 1/110 of the cake. If 10 guests come, then we will give each one a large piece and one small piece - 1/11 + 1/110 = 1/10. If 11 guests come, then ten of them will be given one big piece $($1/11 of the cake$)$, and one guest will get 10 small pieces.

Answer

20 pieces.
