Problem ID 111328

A cinema contains 7 rows each with 10 seats. A group of 50 children went to see the morning screening of a film, and returned for the evening screening. Prove that there will be two children who sat in the same row for both the morning and the evening screening.

Solution

If each row had no more than 7 children sitting in it then the total number of children could be no more than 49. It follows that there is a row which contained no fewer than 8 children at the morning screening. Since there are only 7 rows at least two of these children must have ended up in the same row in the evening screening - by the pigeon hole principle.
