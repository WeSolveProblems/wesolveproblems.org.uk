Problem ID 103802

Which five-digit numbers are there more of: ones that are not divisible by 5 or those with neither the first nor the second digit on the left being a five?

Solution 1

Let's count the number of numbers that are not multiples of 5. For the first digit, you can choose among any of 9 non-zero digits, for the second, third and fourth - any of 10 digits, for the last digit - any of eight $($not 5 and not 0$)$. In total, we get $9 \times 8 \times 10^3$ numbers $($compare with problem number 60366$)$.

Now we count the number of numbers for which neither the first nor the second digit is a five. The first digit can be any of 8 digits $($not 5 and not 0$)$, the second - any of nine $($not 5$)$, the third, fourth and fifth - any of 10 digits. We get the same result: $9 \times 8 \times 10^3$ numbers.

Answer

There is the same number of numbers that are not divisible by 5 and those with neither the first nor the second digit on the left being a five.
