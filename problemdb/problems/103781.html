Problem ID 103781

A pedestrian walked along six streets of one city, passing each street exactly twice, but could not get around them, having passed each one only once. Could this be?

Solution

Consider six streets emerging from the centre of the city in different directions $($that is, six segments with a common start point and without other common points$)$. A pedestrian can, after leaving the centre, go back and forth on each street. But, obviously, it's impossible to walk on every street exactly once.

Answer

Yes, this could be.
