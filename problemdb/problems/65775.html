Remarks

With increasing n, the probability is approximately 1/3. There is an assumption that if we take an ellipse or a circle and raise the question of the probability of crossing two random chords, the answer must be 1/3. Unfortunately, everything is not so simple. It is important how the random chords are chosen, because the chord of the circle is infinitely large, in contrast to the diagonals of the polygon. You can do this, for example, in this way: in some random way we choose four points A, B, C and D on the circle, independently of each other. What is the probability that the chords AB and CD intersect? Here the answer is really 1/3. You can try to prove it.
