Problem ID 57310

a, b and c are the lengths of the sides of an arbitrary triangle. Prove that $a^2 + b^2 + c^2$ < $2 $($ab + bc + ca$)$.

Solution

By the triangle inequality, $a^2 $>$ $($b - c$)$^2$ = $b^2$ - 2bc + $c^2$, $b^2$ $>$ $a^2$ - 2ac + $c^2$ and $c^2$ $>$ $a^2$ - 2ab + $b^2$. Adding these inequalities, we obtain the required result.
