Problem ID 35575

A convex figure and point A inside it are given. Prove that there is a chord $($that is, a segment joining two boundary points of a convex figure$)$ passing through point A and dividing it in half at point A.

Hint

Continuously rotate the chord. Prove that with this rotation we will achieve the desired position.

Solution

Draw some chord through A. We denote by a and b the lengths of the segments to which the chord is divided by point A. Let's begin to rotate the chord anticlockwise $($thereby continuously changing the direction of the chord$)$. In this case, the quantity a depends continuously on the rotation angle $ \phi $, that is $ a = a (\phi) $. In the same way, b continuously depends on the angle of rotation $ \phi $. Consider the difference a-b as a function of $ \phi $. At the initial instant, this quantity is equal to $ a (0) -b (0) $, with a rotation of 1800, the segments to which point A breaks the chord are interchanged, i.e. $ a (\pi) -b (\pi) = - (a (0) -b (0)) $. Thus, at the ends of the segment $ [0; \pi] $ the function $ a (\phi) -b (\phi) $ takes values of different signs, therefore for some $ \phi \in [0; \ pi] $ the equality $ a (\phi) -b (\phi) = 0 $, i.e. equality of the lengths of chord segments is achieved.
