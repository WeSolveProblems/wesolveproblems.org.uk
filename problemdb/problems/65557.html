Problem ID 65557

What is the largest number of horses that can be placed on an $8\times8$ chessboard so that no horse touches more than seven of the others?

Solution

Consider an example of an arrangement of 60 horses: all cells are occupied, except cells of the central square $2\times2$. We can show that, if the condition satisfies the arrangement, at least four cells remain free $($that is, more than 60 horses cannot be arranged$)$. Consider the central square of $4\times4$. If there are four empty cells in it, all is proved.

Let there be no more than three free empty cells in it, then there are at least 13 horses positioned. Each of them touches 8 cells of the board, one of which is free. It is easy to verify that a fixed horse, standing in the central square, can be touched attacked by no more than four horses. Consequently, the number of free cells is no less than 13/ 4 $>$ 3, that is, no less than 4.

Answer

60 horses.
