Problem

A bag contains balls of two different colours - black and white. What is the minimum number of balls you need to remove without looking, to guarantee that within the removed balls, at least two are the same colour.

Solution

In total you need to remove 3 balls. Consider the balls as rabbits, and the colours as hutches. Since there are fewer hutches than rabbits, by the Pigeon Hole principle there will be a hutch that contains at least two rabbits - that is two balls of the same colour. It is easy to see, that if we remove two balls they could be different colours, so the minimum must be 3.

Answer

3 balls
