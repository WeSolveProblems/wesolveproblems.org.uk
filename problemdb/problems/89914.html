Problem ID 89914

Woodchucks are sawing a log. They made 10 cuts. How many pieces were made?

Hint

Into how many parts is the log divided into by the first cut? How does the number of pieces change after each subsequent cut?

Solution

The number of pieces is always 1 more than the number of cuts, because the first cut divides the log into two parts, and each next cut adds another piece.

Answer

11 pieces.
