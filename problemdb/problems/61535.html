Problem ID 61535

Prove that the 13th day of the month is more likely to occur on a Friday than on other days of the week. It is assumed that we live in the Gregorian style calendar.

Hint

Take advantage of the fact that the number of days in the 400-year cycle is divisible by 7.

Solution

Recall that the year is not a leap year if its number is not divisible by 4 and if its number is divisible by 100, but not by 400. A non-leap year consists of 52 weeks and one day, and in a leap year, there is one day more. Thus, the number of days in the 400-year cycle is comparable to 300 + 100 - 3 = 497 (mod 7), that is, divisible by 7. Therefore, it is sufficient to investigate one such cycle, for example, from year 2000 to 2399.

If the 13th day of the month is Friday, then the first day of this month is Sunday. Therefore, we must check that the month mostly starts on a Sunday. We divide the years into 7 types corresponding to the first day of the year: the year of the 1st type starts on a Monday, the second on a Tuesday, ..., the 6th on a Saturday, and the 0th on a Sunday. If a non-leap year is of type n, then the next year is of type n + 1 $($mod 7$)$. If the shaded year is of type n, then the next year is of type n + 2 $($mod 7$)$, and the next leap year is of type n - 2 $($mod 7$)$. For example, as can be seen from the calendar, 2000 is a sixth-type year, 2001 is a non-leap year of type 1, and 2004 is a leap year of type 4. It is easy to verify. that in the non-leap year of type 0, Monday, Tuesday and Thursday will be 1st one time, Friday, Saturday and Sunday - 2 times, and Wednesday - 3 times. In a leap year of type 0, Monday, Tuesday and Friday will be 1st one time, Wednesday, Thursday and Saturday - 2 times, and Sunday - 3 times. In years of other types, a corresponding shift occurs; for example, in a leap year of type 1, Tuesday, Wednesday and Friday will be the 1st number one time, Saturday, Sunday and Monday - 2 times, Thursday - 3 times.

In our 400-year cycle there are two kinds of sub-cycles. First, it is 7 consecutive non-leap years $($for example, 2097 - 2103$)$. It is clear that in such a cycle a non-leap year of each type occurs once. Secondly, these are 28 consecutive years, of which exactly 7 are leap years $($for example, 2000 - 2027$)$. It is not difficult to verify that here, such a leap year of each type occurs once, and a leap year of each type occurs three times. Years included in such cycles can be excluded from consideration and this leaves only 2000 - 2012, 2104 - 2112, 2204 - 2212, 2304 - 2312, 2397 - 2399.

Note that due to the cyclicity, the year 2104 starts on the same day of the week as the 2097th, 2013th, and therefore 2008: from 2008 to 2013 there is a shift by 2 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 2 = 7 days. It means that the period 2104 - 2112 can be replaced by the period 2008 - 2016. Similarly, the periods 2204 - 2212, 2304 - 2312, 2397 - 2399 are replaced by the periods of 2012 - 2020, 2016 - 2024 and 2025 - 2027 respectively. Applying the resulted periods, we get a 28-year cycle of 2000 - 2027 plus a gap of 2008 - 2020 and another for 2012 and 2016.

So, we have in consideration the non-leap years 2009, 2010, 2011, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2017, 2019, that are, respectively, of types 4, 5, 6, 2, 3, 4, 0, 1, 2 and leap years 2008, 2012, 2012 , 2016, 2016, 2020, that are, respectively, of types 2, 0, 0, 5, 5, 3. Removing from non-leap years the types from 0 to 6, we leave only types 2 and 4, which gives 5 numbers for Sundays as the 1st numbers, 4 Tuesdays, 4 Fridays, 3 Mondays, Wednesdays and Thursdays and one Saturday. The leap years add 11 Sundays, Tuesdays and Fridays, 10 Wednesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays, and 9 Mondays. In total, we have, 16 Sundays, 15 Tuesdays and Fridays, 13 Wednesdays and Thursdays and 12 Mondays.

We see that Sunday is the 1st number a little more often than every other day of the week.
