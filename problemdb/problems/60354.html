Problem

There are $2k+1$ cards, numbered with the numbers $1$ to $2k+1$. What is the largest number of cards that can be chosen, so that no number on a chosen card is equal to the sum of two numbers from two other chosen cards?

Solution

If we take all of the cards with odd numbers on them (of which there are $k+1$ the condition will be fulfilled). If we take $k+2$ cards, and subtract all of the other numbers from the largest number $N$, we will get $k+1$ different values. All of the remaining numbers cannot exceed $2k$, therefore at least two of them will coincide with the numbers on the $k+2$ chosen cards. At least one of these two will not be equal to $ \frac{N}{2}$.

Answer

$k+1$ cards.
