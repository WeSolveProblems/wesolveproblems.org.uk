Problem ID 30359

Prove that the product of any three consecutive natural numbers is divisible by 6.

Hint

Show that one of the factors is divisible by 2 and one of the factors is divisible by 3.

Solution

Among these three numbers there is at least one even number.Hence, in the decomposition of the product into prime factors there is a factor of 2.

Among these three numbers, one number is divisible by 3. Hence, in the decomposition of a product into prime factors there is a factor 3.

In the decomposition of the product into prime factors there are the prime numbers 2 and 3. Hence, it is divisible by their product, that is, by 6.
