Problem ID 35438

In a group of friends, each two people have exactly five common acquaintances. Prove that the number of pairs of friends is divisible by 3.

Hint

Express the number of triples of people in pairs through the number of acquaintances.

Solution

Let P denote the number of pairs of familiar people (that is, the number of edges in the corresponding graph which you can draw yourself), and let T be the number of triangles in this graph. By assumption, each of the edges goes into exactly 5 triangles. On the other hand, each of the T triangles contains exactly 3 edges. Consequently, 5P = 3T. Since 3 and 5 are both prime numbers, P is divisible by 3.
