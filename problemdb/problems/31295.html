Problem ID 31295

There are 100 notes of two types: a and b pounds, and a $\neq$ b $($mod 101$)$. Prove that you can select several bills so that the amount received (in pounds) is divisible by 101.

Solution

Let there be m ≤ 50 bills of a pounds and n = 100 - m bills of b pounds. Consider the sums a, a + b, 2a + b, 2a + 2b, 2a + 3b, 3a + 3b, ..., ma + mb, ma + $($m + 1$)$ b, ..., ma + nb. In total, we have 2m + $($n – m$)$ = 100. There are three possibilities.

1) Among these sums there is a multiple of 101. This is the required sum.

2) There are two sums that are congruent modulo 101. Then the required sum is their difference.

3) The remainders from dividing these sums by 101 take all of the values from 1 to 100. Then one of them $($but not a$)$ is comparable with b, modulo 101. Subtracting b from it, we obtain the required sum.

Remarks

In Ivanov's book, it was only required that the numbers a and b were not equal. But then the statement is incorrect: for example, out of 100 denominations of denomination 1 and 102 pounds, you cannot collect an amount that is a multiple of 101.
