Problem ID 97939

Two play a game on a chessboard 8 Ã— 8. The player who makes the first move puts a knight on the board. Then they take turns moving it $($ according to the usual rules $)$, whilst you can not put the knight on a cell which he already visited. The loser is one who has nowhere to go. Who wins with the right strategy - the first player or his partner?

Solution

We divide all the squares into 32 pairs so that the squares of one pair are connected by the course of the knight $($ Figure 1 $)$.

Wherever the first player puts his knight somewhere for his turn, the second player moves it to the cell in its pair. Therefore, the second player will always have a move to make, and he can not lose.

Answer

His partner.
