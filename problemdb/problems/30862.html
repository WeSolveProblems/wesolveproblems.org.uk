Problem ID 30862

Prove that $\frac {1}{2}$ $(x^2 + y^2) $\geq$ xy for any x and y.

Solution

By rearranging the terms, we obtain $\frac {1}{2}$ $($x - y$)$^2 $\geq$ 0.

Remarks

This proves the Cauchy inequality for two positive numbers $($it is sufficient to make the substitution a = $x^2$, b = $y^2$$)$.
