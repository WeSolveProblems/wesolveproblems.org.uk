Problem ID 64305

Author: D.E. Shnol

On the island of Truthland, all of the inhabitants may be mistaken, but the younger ones never contradict the elders, and when the older ones contradict the younger ones, they $($the elders$)$ are not mistaken. Between the residents A, B and C there was such a conversation:

A: B is the tallest.

B: A is the tallest.

C: I'm taller than B.

Does it follow from this conversation that the younger the person, the taller he or she is $($for the three people having this conversation$)$?

Solution

Both B and C contradict A. Therefore, they are older than A, that is, A is the youngest. When the elders contradict the younger ones, they are not mistaken, it means that indeed, A is the tallest, and C is taller than B, that is, B is the shortest. But who is older, B or C, we cannot learn this from this conversation $($they do not contradict each other$)$. It could also be that the shortest B could be the middle one in age, that is, neither the youngest nor the oldest.

Answer

It does not follow.
