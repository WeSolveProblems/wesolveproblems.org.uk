Problem ID 35768

Draw six points on the plane so that they serve as vertices for exactly 17 triangles.

Hint

If no three points lie on one line, then every three points will form a triangle, and there will be only $C_6^3 = 20$ triangles. If you "put" three points on one straight line, then one triangle will be smaller. Hence, we need to create three triples of points lying on one line.

Answer

For example, we can take vertices and midpoints of the sides of an arbitrary triangle.
