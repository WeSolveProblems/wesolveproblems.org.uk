Problem

a) A 1 or a 0 is placed on each vertex of a cube. The sum of the 4 adjacent vertices is written on each face of the cube. Is it possible for each of the numbers written on the faces to be different?

b) The same question, but if 1 and -1 are used instead.

Solution

a) Notice there are 5 possible values that could be on each face $0, 1, 2, 3$ or $4$. But there are a total of 6 faces, meaning that by the Pigeon Hole Principle at least two faces must share a value.

b) The solution is identical, but the 5 possible values are $-4, -2, 0, 2, 4$
