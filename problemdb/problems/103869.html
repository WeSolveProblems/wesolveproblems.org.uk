Problem ID 103869

Solve the puzzle: $BAO \times BA \times B$ = 2002.

Hint

2002 = $2 \times 7 \times 11 \times 13$.

Solution

Note that B = 1. Indeed, if $B \geq 2$, then $BAO \times BA \times B \geq 200 \times 20 \times 2$ = 8000 $>$ 2002.

2002 = $2 \times 7 \times 11 \times 13$. BA is a two-digit divisor of the number 2002, starting with the digit 1, that is, BA can be 11, 13 or $2 \timees 7$ = 14. Since $B \neq A$, then $BA \neq 11$. If BA = 13, BAO = 2002: 13 = 154, from where A is equal to both 3 and 5. This is a contradiction. The remaining option is BA = 14, BAO = 2002/ 14 = 143 is the solution.

Answer

$143 \times 14 \times 1$ = 2002.
