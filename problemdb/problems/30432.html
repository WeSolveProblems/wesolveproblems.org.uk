Problem ID 30432

a) A piece of wire that is 120 cm long is given. Is it possible, without breaking the wire, to make a cube frame with sides of 10 cm?

b) What is the smallest number of times it will be necessary to break the wire in order to still produce the required frame?

Solution

a) If it was possible, then the wire would go along the edges of the cube without overlapping, that is, we would draw the cube frame without taking the pencil off the paper. But this is impossible, since the cube has eight odd vertices.

Answer

a) It is impossible; b) three times.
