Problem ID 60863

Prove that the number $ \ sqrt {2} + \ sqrt {3} + \ sqrt {5} + \ sqrt {7} + \ sqrt {11} + \ sqrt {13} + \ sqrt {17} $ is irrational.

Hint

To solve this problem, it is more convenient to prove a more general statement: if $b_1, ..., b_n$ are non-zero integers and if $a_1, ..., a_n$ are natural numbers that are free from squares, then $b_1 \ sqrt {a_1} + ... + b_n \ sqrt {a_n} \neq 0. (13.5)$

Choosing here $a_1 = 1$, we obtain the irrationality of the sum of the radicals $ \ sqrt {a_2} + ... + \sqrt {a_n} $. To prove the relation 13.5, use induction on the prime numbers $p_1, ..., p_m$ that are prime multiplies of the numbers $a_1, ..., a_n$.
