Problem ID 56673

Two circles $S_1$ and $S_2$ with centers $O_1$ and $O_2$ touch at the point A. A straight line intersects $S_1$ at $A_1$ and $S_2$ at the point $A_2$. Prove that $O_1A_1$ || $O_2A_2$.

Solution

The points $O_1$, A and $O_2$ lie on one line, so $\angle A_2AO_2$ = $\angle A_1AO_1$. The triangles $AO_2A_2$ and $AO_1A_1$ are isosceles, so $\angle A_2AO_2$ = $\angle AA_2O_2$ and $\angle A_1AO_1$ = $\angle AA_1O_1$. Therefore, $\angle AA_2O_2$ = $\angle AA_1O_1$, that is, $O_1A_1$ || $O_2A_2$.
