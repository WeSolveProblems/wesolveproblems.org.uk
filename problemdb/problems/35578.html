Problem ID 35578

In how many ways can you rearrange the numbers from 1 to 100 so that the neighbouring numbers differ by no more than 1?

Hint

Where can the number 1 stand?

Solution

Next to the number 1 only the number 2 can be located, so 1 stands at the beginning or at the end of the sequence. Suppose that 1 is at the beginning. Then the next number is 2, the next number is 3 $($there cannot be other numbers next to 2$)$, the next number is 4, etc. We get the arrangement 1, 2, ..., 99, 100. If 1 is located at the end, then the arrangement 100, 99, ..., 2, 1 is restored unequivocally.

Answer

In two ways.
