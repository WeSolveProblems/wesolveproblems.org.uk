Problem ID 54025

Prove that the segment connecting the vertex of an isosceles triangle to a point lying on the base is no greater than the lateral side of the triangle.

Hint

Let M be a point on the base BC of an isosceles triangle ABC, different from the points B and C. Then one of the angles AMB or AMC is obtuse or equal to 90 degrees.

Solution

Let M be a point on the base of BC of an isosceles triangle ABC, different from points B and C. Then one of the angles AMB and AMC is equal to 90 degrees or is obtuse. Suppose that $\angle AMB \geq 90^{\circ}$. Then this is the largest angle of the triangle AMB. Therefore, AM $<$ AB.
