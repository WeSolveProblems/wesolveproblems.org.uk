Problem ID 35483

Prove that there exist numbers, that can be presented in no fewer than 100 ways in the form of a summation of 20001 terms, each of which is the 2000th power of a whole number

Hint

Consider all of the possible summations consisting of 2001 2000th powers of the first $N$ natural numbers. How many such summations are there?

Solution

Consider the numbers $1, 2, 3, ..., N$. Of the 2000th powers of these numbers we will consider all of the possible summations containing 2001 terms. Without taking order into account there will be no fewer than $ \frac{N^2^0^0^1}{2001!}$ such possible summations. Each term can be chosen $N$ different ways and because of repetition there can be no more than $2001!$ summations.

On the other hand, each of the summations being considered can be no greater than $2001N^2^0^0^0$. Hence if $ \frac{N^2^0^0^1}{2001!}> 100 \times 2001 N^2^0^0^0$, that is N > 100 \times 2001 \times 2001, then no fewer than 100 of the summations must have the same value - which is what is required.
