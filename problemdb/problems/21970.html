Problem

A bag contains balls of two different colours - black and white. What is the minimum number of balls you need to remove, without looking, to guarantee that within the removed balls at least two are the same colour.

Solution

In total you need to remove 3 balls. Consider the balls as pigeons, and the colours as roosting holes. Since there are fewer holes than pigeons, by the Pigeon Hole principle there will be a hole that contains at least two pigeons - that is two balls of the same colour. It is easy to see that if we remove two balls they could be different colours, so the minimum must be 3.

Answer

3 balls
