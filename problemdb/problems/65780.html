Problem ID 65780

Two hockey teams of the same strength agreed that they will play until the total score reaches 10.

Find the mathematical expectation of the number of times when there is a draw.

Solution

If 2n is the maximum total score, then the game can be considered as a random walk of length 2n: at each step, the gap in the score either increases by one, or decreases by one. Let $I_{2k}$ be the indicator of a draw at the 2k-th step:

The random variable X "the number of times when a tie occurred" is equal to the sum of all of the indicators. The beginning of the game will not be considered an "offensive". Therefore, $X = I_2 + I_4 + ... + I_{2n}$, and For n = 5 we obtain EX = 2/4 + 6/16 + 20/64 + 70/256 + 252/2048 ≈ 1.707.

Answer

≈ 1.707.
