Problem

It is known, that a camera located at $O$ cannot see the objects $A$ and $B$, where the angle $AOB$ is greater than $179^\circ$. 1000 such cameras are placed in a (Cartesian) plane. All of the cameras simultaneously take a picture. Prove that there will be a picture taken, in which no more than 998 cameras are visible.

Solution

Assume that in each picture taken by a camera, all 999 other cameras are visible. Therefore the cameras must be located on the vertices of a convex 100-sided polygon (otherwise there will be a camera, from whose picture does not show all of the other cameras). The sum of the angles in a 100-sided polygon is equal to $180^\circ \times 998 = 179640^\circ > 179^\circ \times 1000$. Therefore one of the vertices must have an angle of greater than $179^\circ$. The camera located on this vertex cannot therefore capture all of the other cameras in its picture. This is a contradiction.
