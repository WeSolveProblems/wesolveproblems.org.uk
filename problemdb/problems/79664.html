Problem ID 79664

In a basket, there are 30 red and green apples. Among any 12 apples there is at least one red one, and among any 20 apples there is at least one green one. How many red apples and how many green apples are there in the basket?

Solution

Since among 12 apples there is at least one red one, then there are no more than 11 green apples, and from any 20 apples there is at least one red one, that is, there is no more than 19 green apples. Since there are no more than 30 apples, there are 11 green apples and 19 red apples.
