Problem ID 57368

In the quadrilateral ABCD, the angles A and B are equal, and $\angle$D $>$ $\angle$C. Prove that AD $<$ BC.

Solution

Let $\angle$A = $\angle$B. It is sufficient to prove that if AD $<$ BC, then $\angle$D $>$ $\angle$C. Take the point $D_1$ on the side BC so that $BD_1$ = AD. Then $ABD_1D$ is an isosceles trapezoid. Therefore, $\angleD > \angleD_1DA$ = $\angleDD_1B > \angleC$.
