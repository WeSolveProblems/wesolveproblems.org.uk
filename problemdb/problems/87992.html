Problem ID 87992

One three-digit number consists of different digits that are in ascending order, and in its name all words begin with the same letter. The other three-digit number, on the contrary, consists of identical digits, but in its name all words begin with different letters. What are these numbers?

Hint

Notice that "the numbers are equal" and "the numbers start with the same letter" - these are two completely different statements.

Solution

These numbers are, respectively, 147 and 111. The problem is solved by a simple search of options, of which there are not so many.

Answer

147 and 111.
