Problem ID 30911

Will the entire population of the Earth, all buildings and structures on it, fit into a cube with a side length of 3 kilometres?

Solution

The volume of such a cube is $27 \times 10^9$ $m^3$. The population of the Earth exceeds $7 \times 10^9$ people. Therefore, each person has less than 4 $m^3$. It can be assumed that there are more buildings per person.

Answer

It will not fit.
