Problem ID 35430

Given a board (divided into squares) of the size: a) 10x12, b) 9x10, c) 9x11, consider the game with two players where: in one turn a player is allowed to cross out any row or any column if there is at least one square not crossed out. The loser is the one who cannot make a move. Is there a winning strategy for one of the players?

Hint

Keep track of the parity (whether it is even or not) of the number of rows and columns.

Solution

a) A strategy for the second player: always leave an even number of rows not crossed out and an even number of columns not crossed out. Then the second player can always make a move.
b) A strategy for the first player: during their first turn, cross out a row in order to make the board be 8x10. Then proceed by the strategy from a).
c) A strategy for the second player: after the first player's first turn, make the board be 8x10 (if the first player crosses out a row, then te second player crosses out a column and vice versa). Then proceed to play using the strategy from a).

Answer

a), c) There is a winning strategy for the second player. b) There is a winning strategy for the first player.
