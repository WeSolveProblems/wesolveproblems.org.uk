Problem ID 103873

Jessica, Nicole and Alex received 6 coins between them: 3 gold coins and 3 silver coins. Each of them received 2 coins. Jessica doesn't know which coins the others received but only which coins she has. Think of a question which Jessica can answer with either "yes", "no" or "I don't know" such that from the answer you can know which coins Jessica has.

Solution

Let us check if the question "Is it true that you have more gold coins than Alex?" fits the conditions.
If Jessica has 2 gold coins then she can answer "yes" since Alex cannot have more than 1 gold coin. If Jessica has 2 silver coins then Alex must have at least 1 gold coin and so Jessica would answer "no". If Jessica received 1 gold coin and 1 silver coin then she would answer "I don't know", since Alex might have either 2 gold coins or 2 silver coins.
Of course, other questions can also fit the requirements, such as:
"Is it true that one of the others received 2 silver coins?"
"Is it true that the others each received at least 1 gold coin?"
"If I take 1 coin from you and give you a gold coin instead, would you have more gold coins now?"
(Notice that in the last question we do not refer to the coins received by Nicole and Alex, but only to those received by Jessica.)

Answer

For example: "Is it true that you have more gold coins than Alex?".
