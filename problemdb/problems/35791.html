Problem ID 35791

Sam and Lena have several chocolates, each weighing not more than 100 grams. No matter how they share these chocolates, one of them will have a total weight of chocolate that does not exceed 100 grams. What is the maximum total weight of all of the chocolates?

Hint

It is possible to divide the chocolates "almost equally" - so that the difference between the weights of Lena’s and Sam’s chocolates does not exceed 100 grams.

Solution

Let's give all of the chocolates to Sam and then, we will give one chocolate to Lena. At first, the weight of Sam’s chocolates is greater, but there will come a time when Lena's chocolates will weigh no less than Sam’s. Let Sam’s chocolates weigh A g at this point $($by condition A $\leq$ 100$)$, Lena’s chocolates weigh B g, and the last chocolate that we transferred, weighed C g. Before the transfer, the total weight of Lena’s B-C chocolates was not more than 100g. Thus, the total weight of all of the chocolates is $A + (B-C) + C \leq 3 \times 100$ = 300g.

Answer

300 grams.
