Problem ID 65324

At the sound of the whistle of the PE teacher, all 10 boys and 7 girls lined up randomly. Find the mathematical expectation of the value "the number of girls standing to the left of all of the boys."

Solution

Add one more boy mentally, putting him to the right of all. Then the whole line is divided into 11 groups, each of which ends with a boy. The smallest group consists of one boy, the largest can consist of 8 people - 7 girls and one boy.

We number these groups from left to right and call $ξ_k$ the number of the k-th group. We are interested in the expected number of the first group, that is, $Eξ_1$.

The sum of all $ξ_k$ values is 18 $($17 current schoolchildren and one conceptual boy$)$: $ξ_1 + ξ_2 + ... + ξ_11$ = 18 $($*$)$. By chance, all of the quantities $ξ_k$ are equally distributed, so their expectations are equal to each other and equal to $Eξ_1$.

Going from the equality $($*$)$ to the expectations, we get: $11Eξ_1 = 18$, from which $Eξ_1 = 18/11$. The expected number of girls per unit is 7/11.

Answer

7/11.
