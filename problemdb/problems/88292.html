Problem ID 88292

The vendor has a cup weighing scales with unequal shoulders and weights. First he weighs the goods on one cup, then on the other, and takes the average weight. Is he deceiving customers?

Solution

Suppose we put an item weighing 1 kg on the left-hand side of the scale, and weight weighing a kg in the other bowl for balance, that is, a times more. If we transfer the goods to the right cup, then for equilibrium on the left we will have to put a weight a times smaller, that is 1 / a kg. Thus, the seller will take the amount of money for kg, which, according to the Cauchy inequality, is more than 1 kg.

Answer

Yes he deceives customers.
